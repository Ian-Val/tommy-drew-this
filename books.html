The Boatman

Thomas M. Drew & Craig Medeiros

A boy.  A building.  A boatman.

There are many stories about the eccentric old man in the rain slicker, roaming around the building in search of his lost boat.

But a curious boy learns the Boatman is more extraordinary, and far simpler, than anyone bothers to realize.

The Boatman is a parable about the search for truth and understanding in tumultuous times.

Night of the Jangler

Thomas M. Drew

A night of trick 'r' treating takes a terrifying turn when three mischief-making kids, out looking for adventure, find themselves on the wrong side of abandoned railroad tracks.

After hearing a tale about an old ghost train, these “Three Halloweeners with mischievous demeanors” can’t help but to try and see it for themselves. What they find is far scarier than anything their imaginations have prepared them for.

A celebration of Halloween and all things that go bump in the night, Night of the Jangler is an allegorical rhyme about facing our fears and the masks they hide behind.
